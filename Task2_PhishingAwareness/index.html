Phishing Awareness Training

What is Phishing?

Phishing is a type of cyberattack where attackers impersonate a trusted source (like a bank, company, or coworker) to trick people into revealing sensitive information: passwords, credit card numbers, or personal data, or into installing malware.

Phishing usually arrives through email, but can also come via text messages (smishing), phone calls (vishing), or fake websites that look identical to real ones.

Common Social Engineering Tactics

Urgency

Creating a false sense of time pressure ("act now or lose access") so victims don't stop to think.

Authority

Pretending to be a boss, bank, or government agency to make requests seem legitimate.

Fear

Threatening consequences like account suspension, legal action, or fines.

Curiosity / Reward

Offering prizes, refunds, or exciting attachments to lure clicks.

Best Practices to Avoid Phishing

  • Always check the sender's email address carefully, not just the display name.
  • Hover over links to preview the real URL before clicking.
  • Be suspicious of urgent or threatening language demanding immediate action.
  • Never share passwords, OTPs, or card details via email or text.
  • If unsure, contact the company directly using official contact details: not info from the email.
  • Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) on your important accounts.
  • Keep your software and browser updated to patch security vulnerabilities.
